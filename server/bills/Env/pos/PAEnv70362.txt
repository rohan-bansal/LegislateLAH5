Highlights: Establishes the Per-and-Polyfluoroalkyl Substances Remediation Program to be administered by the Pennsylvania Infrastructure Investment Authority (PennVEST) (Sec. 3). Specifies that the program is meant to remediate the presence of per-and-polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) in drinking water which are not related to the presence of a former military installation (Sec. 3). Requires qualified municipalities to submit an application in order to receive benefits from the program (Sec. 3). Specifies that the program shall be administered using funds made available to PennVEST (Sec. 3). Establishes the Military Installation Remediation Fund, which will contain restricted accounts for each former military installation (Sec. 3). Specifies that the money in the restricted accounts shall be used to offset any costs imposed on customers of a water provider relating to the removal of PFAS in drinking water due to the presence of a former military installation (Sec. 3). Specifies that transfers to a special fund under this act shall not exceed 500 percent of the local taxes designated to the municipality during the year (Sec. 3). Specifies that an approved application under this section shall be in effect for a period no later than 30 years from its effective date (Sec. 3).
