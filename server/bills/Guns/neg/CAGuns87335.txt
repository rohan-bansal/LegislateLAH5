Highlights: Alleges threat of proliferation of firearms among minors (Sec. 1). Prohibits marketing firearms in a manner designed, intended, or reasonably appearing to be attractive to minors (Sec. 2). Defines examples of marketing considered “attractive” to minors (e.g. use of cartoon characters, toys, games) (Sec. 2). Establishes civil penalty of up to $25,000 per violation; authorizes individuals to sue (Sec. 2).
